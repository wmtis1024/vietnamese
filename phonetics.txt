Vietnamese has 8 tones
======================

sắc

nặng same as 5 in cantonese （我 你）
ngã

má mà mả mã mạ ma

2 nhập thanh (入聲) ends in -p, -t, -c, -ch
cấp same as 1 (7) in cantonese （級）
nhập same as 6 (8) in cantonese （入）

Consonents
==========
b, c, ch, d, đ, g/gh, gi, h, k, kh, l, m, n, ng/ngh, nh, p, ph, qu, r, s, t, th, tr, v, x

gi, d, v sound like Y in English in southern Vietnamese
gi, d sounds like Z in English in northern Vietnamese
v sounds like V in English in northern Vietnamese

Same as cantonese:
ch (執)
k (急)
kh (級)
ng (外)
th (台)

Not in cantonese:
qu (chính quyền 政權)
t (tạm biệt 暫別)
tr (trung quốc 中國)
r (rẻ)
nh (đại nhân 大人)

Vowels
======

a, ă, â, e, ê, i, o, ô, ơ, u, ư, y

Same as cantonese:
ă read as á because it is a short vowel and cannot be pronouced alone săn (音心)
e lẹ (寫)
i/y mỹ 美 (時)
o bò (可何學)
ơ hoà hợp 和合 (香)
u củ (古)

Not in cantonese:
a (ma)
â read as ớ because it is a short vowel and cannot be pronouced alone (bất) 不
ê (mỹ lệ) 美麗
ô (lộ) 路 tiết lộ 洩露   s (Cantonese) --> t, th (Vietnamese)
ư (sư phụ) 師父
ươ (hương cảng) 香港
